Bill of Materials — 20' Classic Gaff Sloop (default sizes)

Sailcloth
- Dacron cruising cloth (6.6 oz/yd² recommended)
  - Estimated area: ~310 ft² (28.8 m²)
  - Cloth width typical: 60" (5 ft / 1.52 m)
  - Required length (allowing 15% waste): ≈ 24 yards (22 m) total for both mainsail and jib

Thread & sewing supplies
- UV-resistant bonded polyester thread (T-69 or equivalent), heavy weight for Dacron (2–3 cones)
- Reinforcement webbing (1" / 25 mm polyester webbing): 6–8 m
- Batten pocket webbing and batten end caps

Hardware
- Reef cringles (2 per reef per side) — stainless steel grommets or brass with internal back plates — size to fit 1/4"–3/8" shackles
- Clew pad-eye / stainless pad-eye and shackles (1)
- Headboard fittings (soft or hard) for throat and peak halyards (2)
- Hanks or luff tape slides for jib (if using hanks: 8–12 hanks)
- Halyards: braided polyester 8–10 mm — main throat halyard, peak halyard, jib halyard
- Sheets: 10–12 mm braided polyester for jib & mainsheet
- Battens: 3 full battens — lengths per pattern (fiberglass or composite)
- Bolt rope (if chosen): 14–16 mm dacron/nylon luff/foot rope — length to match luff/foot plus waste
- Grommets & washers (stainless) for reef points and clew/tack/head — quantity ~8–12

Other
- Sail tape (UV protective) for leech & foot edges
- Sail cloth glue or seam tape (optional, for temporary assembly)
- Batten cars, toping lifts and batten ends
